In classic microeconomics there are two agents:
    - Companies
    - Individuals

Company:  
        - Produce goods and services from production factors
        (Individuals and machinery). 
        - Choose how much goods to offer to the market and 
        how many production factors to demand from the market.
        - Maximization of profits is the ultimate goal for a firm.

Individuals:
        - Are rational, they always choose the most optimal solution
        for the given informations and limits.
        - Maximization of utility is the ultimal goal for an individual

Market: 
        - Set of trading actions related to some good or service.
